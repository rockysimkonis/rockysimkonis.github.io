Looping in Ruby vs. Looping in JavaScript

  • Rocky S.
  • Feb 14, 2016
  • Sharable links

From a visual point of view, one of the first differences someones see's between Ruby and Javascript is that Ruby does not use cury brackets nor semi colons. Now if you dig deeper you can find plenty more differences especially when it comes to looping between both languages.

In Ruby there are two methods of iterating through arrays or enum objects. One is 'for' loop or 'in' loop and the other method is using iterators and block. Javacript on the other hand sticks with just 'for' or 'in' loops and does not iterate through arrays as smoothly.
